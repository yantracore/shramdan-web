# Shramdaan Project Summary

## Version Context

Version 1.0 is the first cleanup campaign's complete visible feature set: from issue listing to publishing the final campaign results.

## Purpose

Shramdaan is a community action platform for Nepal. It helps people identify local problems that can be solved through community labor, time, funds, and coordination without waiting for direct government intervention.

The project is not only about reporting problems. It is about turning visible local issues into organized community campaigns, completing the work, and sharing the result publicly so trust and momentum can grow.

## Community Philosophy

Shramdaan is built around a simple belief: many local problems become solvable when people can see them clearly, agree that they matter, and join a practical effort to fix them.

The platform should encourage:

- Shared ownership of local public spaces.
- Practical action over passive complaint.
- Transparent use of labor, money, materials, and time.
- Local pride through visible before-and-after outcomes.
- Replicable community campaigns across Nepal.

## Problem Space

Initial issues may include:

- Waste buildup in public spaces. 
- Polluted roadsides, parks, riverbanks, and neighborhood areas.
- Public spaces that need cleaning, repair, or basic improvement.
- Community places that need collective volunteer support.
- Local problems where people can contribute directly.

The first product focus is cleanup-related work. Future versions may expand into other forms of community service.

## Target Audience

Shramdaan serves:

- Local residents who notice public problems.
- Community members who want to support or vote on important issues.
- Volunteers who can donate labor and time.
- Donors who can contribute funds, materials, or logistics.
- Campaign organizers who coordinate the actual event.
- Public visitors who want to see completed impact stories.

## Core Loop

The core Shramdaan loop is:

1. Identify a local issue.
2. List the issue publicly.
3. Gather community support through votes or interest.
4. Promote high-attention issues into cleanup campaigns.
5. Invite nearby and interested people to participate.
6. Collect labor, time, funds, materials, or logistical support.
7. Complete the campaign.
8. Publish the result with proof, summary, and impact story.

## Product Positioning

Shramdaan should feel trustworthy, local, practical, and hopeful. It should make community action look achievable and organized without making the experience feel bureaucratic.
